About Us

We are a pair of Computer Science undergraduate students from the University of Toronto with a passion for technology in the healthcare and medical industries. With the unfortunate COVID-19 pandemic having devastated nations all around the world and overwhelming their healthcare systems, we wished to get involved in the public health efforts to deliver treatment to those who need it most in any way we could, which led to the creation of this project.

Given how dire the infection crisis has become in India, seeing more than 3 lakh cases daily at its worst, there has been an emergency acceptance of the experimental treatment known as Convalescent Plasma Therapy.

Convalescent Plasma therapy makes use of the blood of individuals who have already recovered from the illness, donating it to patients who currently have it. The plasma and antibodies against the virus are extracted from the donor’s blood to be given to the patient, with the idea that the antibodies will help mount an effective defense against the active virus in the patient, helping them recover from a severe illness, or preventing the illness from becoming severe. However, donors should generally have only fully recovered from the illness no more than 3 months (ideally 2 months) before the date of donation, to ensure there is still a high content of antibodies in the blood.

It is worth noting that it is not yet certain how effective this treatment is. It may not provide a very strong defense against the progression of COVID-19, but considering the novelty and severity of this disease, as well as the high level safety of this treatment overall, it is certainly a worthwhile measure that can be taken to avoid severe illness.
